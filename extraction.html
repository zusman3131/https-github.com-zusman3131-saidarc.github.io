EXTRACTION

Get Out Alive

Master the art of extraction: when to leave, how to survive, and how to keep your loot

The Golden Rule

"The best raid is the one where you leave with something. A full backpack is worthless if you never make it to the extract."

Extraction is what separates successful raiders from frustrated ones. It's not about clearing every building or killing every ARC — it's about knowing when you've got enough and getting out safely.

01

Plan Before You Enter

Before deploying, identify 2-3 possible extraction points on your route. Know your primary extract and a backup.

02

Set a Personal Timer

Decide your maximum raid time before deploying. When the timer hits 15 minutes, start moving toward extract regardless of loot.

03

Know When to Cut Losses

If you've lost most of your health or ammo, extract immediately. A small win beats a total loss.

04

Use Smoke Grenades

Always carry smokes. They're your best friend at extraction points, providing concealment while you wait for the ship.

How Extraction Works

📡

1. Locate Extraction Point

Find one of the extraction points on the map. They're marked with special icons and usually in fixed locations.

↓
📻

2. Call for Extraction

Interact with the extraction beacon/radio to call for pickup. This alerts nearby players to your position.

↓
⏱️

3. Wait for Arrival

The extraction vehicle takes 60-90 seconds to arrive. This is the most dangerous period — stay alert!

↓
🚁

4. Board and Escape

Once the vehicle arrives, board it within the time limit. You'll see a countdown — don't miss it!

Pro Extraction Strategies

🌫️

The Smoke Screen

Pop a smoke grenade at the extraction point before calling, then another when the ship arrives. The smoke conceals your position and makes it harder for snipers to target you.

Pro Tip: Throw smokes between you and likely enemy positions, not just at your feet.
👁️

Scout Before Calling

Clear the area around the extraction point before activating the beacon. Check rooftops, nearby buildings, and blind corners. Move on if it feels hot.

Pro Tip: Use the "wait and watch" approach — observe the extract for 30 seconds before calling.
🏃

The Fake-Out

Call extraction at one point, then move to a different one. This baits ambushers and gives you a safer exit. Works best with a team covering both points.

Pro Tip: Communication is key — make sure your team knows the plan.
🤝

Buddy System

If playing with a squad, designate one player to board first while others provide covering fire. Last man boards while teammates watch angles.

Pro Tip: The last person to board should have the most valuable loot in their safe pockets.
🎒>

Safe Pocket Priority

Always keep your most valuable items in safe pockets. If you die, these items are protected. Blueprints, keys, and rare attachments go here first.

Priority Order: Blueprints > Keys > Rare Attachments > Valuable Sellables
🧠>

Know When to Walk Away

If you hear multiple firefights at your extract, or see smoke/flashbangs, rotate to your backup extract. The loot you have is better than the loot you might get.

Remember: "A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush."

Risk vs. Reward

When to push for more, when to extract

🟢 Extract NOW If...

  • You have valuable loot (blueprints, keys, rare items)
  • Your health is below 50%
  • You're low on ammo or healing
  • You've been in raid for 20+ minutes
  • You've heard multiple player firefights nearby
  • It's a Night Raid or Hurricane event (higher risk)
  • You're solo and encounter a squad

🟡 Maybe Push If...

  • Your backpack has lots of empty space
  • You're near a high-value POI with clear entry
  • Your team is full strength with good gear
  • You've seen no player activity for 10+ minutes
  • You have a specific item quest to complete
  • You spawned far from your target loot area
