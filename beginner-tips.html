Beginner's Survival Guide

Essential tips to survive your first raids in the Rust Belt

Your First 10 Raids

A step-by-step guide to getting started

1

Start on Dam Battlegrounds

This is the easiest map with manageable enemies and good loot distribution. The intuitive layout makes it perfect for learning the basics before moving to harder maps.

Pro Tip: Learn the Research & Admin building layout first - it's a great source of early batteries and attachments.
2

Use the Rattler + Ferro Loadout

The Rattler is cheap and reliable for close-to-mid range combat. The Ferro packs a punch for taking down ARCs and handling longer shots. Total cost: approximately 2,000 coins.

Pro Tip: Both weapons use medium ammo, simplifying your inventory management.
3

Bring 240 Medium + 120 Heavy Ammo

This gives you enough ammunition for extended fights without breaking the bank. Adjust based on your specific weapons - heavy ammo is more effective against ARC robots.

4

Learn One Extraction Route

Pick one route and run it until you know it by heart. A good starter route: Control Tower → Water Treatment → North Extract.

Remember: Holster your weapon (Press 5 or H) to run significantly faster to extraction.
5

Prioritize Crafting Table Level 1

This unlocks the Looting Mk. 2 backpack with TWO safe pocket slots. This is game-changing for protecting your most valuable loot from being lost on death.

6

Aim for ARC Knees

The knees are weak points on most ARC units. Hitting them deals critical damage and can stagger the enemy, giving you time to reposition or finish them off.

7

Always Carry Smoke Grenades

Smoke is cheap and invaluable for extractions, reviving teammates, and escaping bad situations. Never leave base without at least 2 smoke grenades.

8

Don't Sell Your Keys

Keys sell for 100 coins, but keeping them lets you farm locked rooms for much better rewards. The loot inside is almost always worth more than the quick sell.

9

Extract With Something

A successful extract with basic loot is better than dying with a full backpack. Know when to walk away — extracting alive means keeping everything you've gathered.

Important: You can extract while knocked down — crawl to the elevator and activate the console!
10

Use Voice Chat

Most players are friendly. Coordinating extractions, sharing loot, and teaming up against ARCs increases everyone's survival rate. Don't be afraid to communicate!

Essential Survival Tips

🎯 Movement Tips

  • Holster to run faster — Press 5 (or H) to holster your weapon and sprint significantly faster. Essential for escaping firefights.
  • Slide for cover — Hold C to slide. Great for reaching cover quickly and moving under obstacles.
  • Slide down ladders — Hold Shift while on a ladder to slide down quickly.
  • Vault and ledge grab — Press Space to vault on most surfaces and grab ledges mid-fall to prevent fall damage.
  • Switch shoulder view — Press X to switch which shoulder you aim over for better corner peeking.
  • Ping everything — Middle mouse button marks enemies, loot, and points of interest for your squad.

⚔️ Combat Tips

  • Bring more than you need — Always bring more ammo, shield rechargers, and bandages than you think you need.
  • Don't underestimate weak ARCs — Even basic enemies can mess you up in bad situations.
  • Clear before looting — Clear areas before looting to avoid getting third-partied.
  • Reposition after fights — After gunfights, assume someone heard you and move to a new position.
  • Use flashlight in dark areas — Press F to toggle flashlight and spot loot/enemies in dark interiors.
  • Heavy ammo for ARCs — Heavy ammunition is more effective against ARC robots.

📈 Progression Tips

  • Start with Free Loadouts — Minimize risk while learning the game mechanics.
  • Prioritize Workshop upgrades — Unlock better crafting to improve your gear over time.
  • Mark quest items — Use the "Keep" action on quest items so you don't accidentally sell them.
  • Focus on Mobility skills first — Marathon Runner and Youthful Lungs (Mobility tree) should be your first 10 skill points.
  • Complete vendor quests — Quests give rewards and help you progress through the game.

🌿 Environmental Awareness

  • Watch the birds — Birds take off when players pass nearby. Use this to track enemies or set traps.
  • ARC patrol patterns — ARCs patrol in predictable loops. Learn their routes to avoid or ambush them.
  • Sprinting attracts attention — Your footsteps and sprinting make noise that attracts ARCs.
  • Baron Husks — Can be looted for materials and blueprints. Keep an eye out for these environmental loot sources.

🎒 Advanced Tricks

  • Zipline with Raider Tool — Your melee tool can hook ziplines for travel without extra gadgets.
  • Throw crafting parts — Fireball Burner and Hornet Driver can be thrown for elemental effects (flames, shocks).
  • Raider Hatch Keys — Buy from vendors and keep in Safe Pocket for guaranteed stealth exits.
  • Smart recycling — Power Cables and Polluted Air Filters are better recycled than sold whole.

👥 Forming a Squad

  • Add friends via Embark IDs
  • Use "Fill Squad" to match with random players
  • Enable crossplay to match with other systems

🗺️ Coordination

  • All party members can see active map conditions while in Speranza
  • Use pings (middle mouse) to mark enemies and loot
  • Voice chat is invaluable - most players are friendly!
⚠️

Don't Get Greedy

Extracting alive is usually better than losing everything. Especially early on, extracting with some loot and progress is safer than risking it all for potential small bonuses.
